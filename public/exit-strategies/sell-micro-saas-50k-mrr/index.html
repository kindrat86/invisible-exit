Revenue-Level Exit Guide

Sell a Micro-SaaS at $50K MRR

At $50K MRR, micro-SaaS exits attract bigger multiples (4-7x), strategic buyers, and complex deal structures. Seller financing, earnouts, and the metrics that drive premium multiples.

TL;DR: At $50k MRR, expect multiples of 4–7x ARR ($2.4M–$4.2M) with a 4-6 months typical. The most important metric at this tier: Growth rate + net dollar retention.

Valuation Multiple
4–7x ARR ($2.4M–$4.2M)
Buyer Profile
Mid-market PE firms, strategic buyers (competitors acquiring for consolidation), family offices. At this tier, buyers often bring their own operational team and are underwriting a 3-5 year hold with a growth plan baked into their model.
Timeline
4-6 months typical. Due diligence is heavier — expect a QoE (quality of earnings) review. Retaining an M&A advisor or broker who specializes in SaaS is standard at this valuation.
Key Metric
Growth rate + net dollar retention. A product growing 30%+ YoY with NDR >100% commands the top of the multiple range. Churn under 2% monthly is table stakes.

Tax Implications

Deal structure matters enormously here. Stock sale vs asset sale can swing your after-tax proceeds by 20-30%. QSBS is the holy grail — if you qualify, the first $10M is tax-free. Most sellers at this tier engage a tax strategist 6-12 months before listing.

At $50K MRR, you have graduated from 'micro-SaaS' to 'small SaaS' in the eyes of institutional buyers. The sale process looks more like a traditional M&A deal: NDA → teaser → IOI → LOI → due diligence → close. You will likely be asked to stay on for a 6-12 month transition, and a portion of the purchase price (typically 20-30%) will be in an earnout tied to post-close performance.
