Side Business for Accountant / CPAs — Risk & Stealth Guide

Can Accountant / CPAs build a side business while employed? Complete non-compete analysis, salary replacement targets, and stealth strategies for 2026.

Risk Level: Medium at Big 4, low elsewhere. The main restriction is client conflict and non-solicitation — don't sell your product to your firm's clients or use your firm's client list. Building a SaaS for a different audience is safe.

Salary & Replacement Target

Median Accountant / CPA Salary: $85,000 (median, non-Big 4)
Monthly Replacement Target: $7,083/mo

Your side business needs to replace this amount to achieve financial independence from your employer. Use the freedom number calculator to model your exit timeline.

Non-Compete Enforceability

Accounting firms (especially Big 4) have some of the strictest non-compete and non-solicitation agreements. However, most are scoped to client service — building a SaaS product that doesn't serve the firm's clients is generally permitted. The AICPA ethics rules don't restrict ancillary businesses.

Safest States: California, Colorado, D.C. offer strongest employee protections. Big 4 accountants in these states have more freedom to build side businesses.

How to Stay Invisible

Build tax-adjacent or accounting-adjacent tools for consumers and small businesses: tax estimators, expense trackers, receipt scanners, bookkeeping automation. These leverage your CPA skills without competing with your firm's core audit/tax services. Never use client data or firm methodologies.

Best Micro-SaaS Ideas for Accountant / CPAs

Tax software for niche audiences (freelancers, creators, digital nomads), expense tracking SaaS, bookkeeping automation, financial planning calculators. Accountants have the credibility to sell financial products that generalist founders can't.

Tax Entity Recommendation

LLC. CPAs should check state board rules on ancillary businesses (most don't restrict non-attest SaaS products). S-Corp election above $50K profit is standard.

Disclaimer: This is educational content, not legal advice. Non-compete laws vary by state and individual employment contract. Consult an employment attorney in your state before starting a side business.
