Side Business for Lawyers — Risk & Stealth Guide

Can Lawyers build a side business while employed? Complete non-compete analysis, salary replacement targets, and stealth strategies for 2026.

Risk Level: Very low. The ABA's ethical rules effectively ban employer non-competes for lawyers. The real risk is client conflict — don't build a legal-tech product that serves your firm's clients or uses your firm's work product.

Salary & Replacement Target

Median Lawyer Salary: $145,000 (median, non-biglaw)
Monthly Replacement Target: $12,083/mo

Your side business needs to replace this amount to achieve financial independence from your employer. Use the freedom number calculator to model your exit timeline.

Non-Compete Enforceability

Lawyers have the strongest protections of any profession. ABA Model Rule 5.6 prohibits non-compete agreements that restrict a lawyer's right to practice. Most states have adopted this rule — non-competes for lawyers are unenforceable in nearly all jurisdictions. Partnership agreements are an exception (they can restrict post-departure practice within the partnership context).

Safest States: All 50 states for employed lawyers. The ABA rule applies universally. Only equity partners face meaningful restrictions.

How to Stay Invisible

Build legal-adjacent tools for consumers (not lawyers): DIY legal forms, compliance checklists, contract templates, dispute-resolution tools. These serve a mass market without competing with your firm. Your domain expertise is a unique moat few competitors have.

Best Micro-SaaS Ideas for Lawyers

Legal template marketplaces, compliance SaaS, document automation for niche industries (construction liens, healthcare HIPAA, education FERPA). Lawyers can build products with genuine regulatory depth that generalist founders can't replicate.

Tax Entity Recommendation

LLC. Most lawyer-founders use a PLLC (Professional LLC) in states that require it. Check your state bar rules on ancillary businesses.

Disclaimer: This is educational content, not legal advice. Non-compete laws vary by state and individual employment contract. Consult an employment attorney in your state before starting a side business.
