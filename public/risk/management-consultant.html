Side Business for Management Consultants — Risk & Stealth Guide

Can Management Consultants build a side business while employed? Complete non-compete analysis, salary replacement targets, and stealth strategies for 2026.

Risk Level: Medium. The biggest risk is client conflict — building a micro-SaaS that serves a client's industry creates a conflict. Avoid any business that overlaps with current or former client engagements.

Salary & Replacement Target

Median Management Consultant Salary: $165,000 (median, MBB/Big 4)
Monthly Replacement Target: $13,750/mo

Your side business needs to replace this amount to achieve financial independence from your employer. Use the freedom number calculator to model your exit timeline.

Non-Compete Enforceability

Heavily dependent on firm and state. Most consulting firms have non-compete clauses in employment agreements, but many are geographically scoped (not blanket bans). Outside of senior partner level, enforcement for side businesses is rare.

Safest States: Same as above. Consultants in California and D.C. have the strongest protections.

How to Stay Invisible

Build B2C products (not B2B) — your employer and clients are B2B-only. Options: content/creator businesses, consumer SaaS, marketplaces for consumers. Use a pseudonym and separate entity. Never mention your firm in any marketing.

Best Micro-SaaS Ideas for Management Consultants

Consumer subscription tools, content-as-a-service, marketplaces, D2C brands. Consultants have deep industry knowledge — apply it to consumers, not businesses, to avoid conflicts.

Tax Entity Recommendation

LLC. At consultant-level income, S-Corp election is critical for self-employment tax savings.

Disclaimer: This is educational content, not legal advice. Non-compete laws vary by state and individual employment contract. Consult an employment attorney in your state before starting a side business.
